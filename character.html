There was conflict, but no narrative; no story.

The First War waged among the primitive meaning-makers, between the Semioticists and the Teleomancers, and language – which many think is the root of magic itself – was borne of that war.

They needed to weaponize the meanings they were creating, give them ontological status, make them persistent, give them virality.

War is the mother of necessity, and necessity the mother of invention, even of language.
